## Research Brief

Between 1 and 6 months, infants transition from reflexive behavior to active exploration. Key cognitive developments include improved visual tracking, emerging object awareness, early cause-and-effect understanding, and the beginnings of memory formation. Habituation studies reveal that infants in this period can distinguish novel from familiar stimuli, prefer human faces, and begin to anticipate routines.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Violation-of-expectation studies show implicit knowledge, not the same as explicit understanding
- Visual tracking milestones vary; premature infants should be assessed at adjusted age
- "Not meeting a milestone" at one visit is not the same as a diagnosis — patterns across visits matter
- Screen time is not recommended for this age group (AAP)
