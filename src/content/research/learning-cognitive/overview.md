## Research Brief

Cognitive development encompasses how children learn to think, reason, remember, and solve problems from birth through adolescence. Major frameworks (Piaget, Vygotsky, information-processing theory) describe overlapping but distinct trajectories. Current guidelines from the AAP, CDC, and WHO emphasize developmental surveillance, responsive caregiving, and early intervention as key levers for supporting healthy cognitive growth.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Cognitive milestones have less normative data than motor milestones — be careful presenting rigid timelines
- Piaget's stages are approximate; modern research shows more gradual and earlier competencies
- Cultural variation exists — milestones were primarily validated in Western populations
- "Delay" does not mean "disorder" — context and pattern matter more than individual milestones
