## Research Brief

Preschool routines become more complex and increasingly involve child participation — morning routines, potty training logistics, preschool preparation, chore introduction, and visual schedules all become central. This is when children begin to internalize routines and take ownership of their daily structure.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: Morning and Evening Routines
- Key points: Reduce stress, build independence, child participation, visual schedules
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: A morning routine chart on the wall turns "Hurry up!" into "Check your chart"

### Section 2: Potty Training Logistics
- Key points: Readiness signs at 18-36 months, child-led approach, specific logistics
- Supported by: Source 2
- Parent-facing angle: They'll do it when they're ready. Your job is to recognize readiness, not force a timeline.

### Section 3: Introducing Chores
- Key points: Start at 3-4, age-appropriate tasks, "helper" identity, predicts adult responsibility
- Supported by: Source 4
- Parent-facing angle: A 3-year-old who puts their plate in the sink is building a lifelong habit

### Section 4: Preschool Preparation
- Key points: Visit the school, practice routines, practice separation, read about it
- Supported by: Source 5
- Parent-facing angle: The more familiar preschool feels before day one, the less scary it is on day one

### Section 5: Screen Time and Bedtime Routines
- Key points: Screen routine > ad-hoc, bedtime routine expands with participation
- Supported by: Source 6, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: "Screen time is from 4:00 to 4:30" ends the negotiation before it starts

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Potty training timing is culturally variable — Chinese families may start earlier (sometimes before 12 months with elimination communication)
- Chinese preschool preparation may include more academic readiness activities
- Chore expectations differ by culture — Chinese families may not introduce chores until later
- Visual schedules work across cultures but the format may need adaptation
