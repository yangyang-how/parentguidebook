## Research Brief

Ages 6 months to 2 years is when most primary teeth erupt — from the first incisors through the first molars. This is also the highest-risk window for early childhood caries (ECC). The article covers tooth eruption progression, establishing brushing routines, the dental home, bottle/sippy cup practices, and the critical role of fluoride during this period.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Nighttime breastfeeding after 12 months and dental risk is a sensitive topic — frame it as one risk factor among many, not as anti-breastfeeding
- Sippy cups with juice are a major hidden cavity source — many parents don't realize
- White spots on teeth (demineralization) are the earliest visible sign of decay — reversible if caught
- Cultural note: In Chinese families, grandparents often give toddlers sweetened drinks — the article should address this without blaming
