## Research Brief

Children aged 2-5 years face a mix of ongoing eczema management (89% of AD cases begin before age 5), increased exposure to skin infections through daycare and preschool (impetigo peaks at 2-6 years), and common viral skin conditions like warts and molluscum. Hives (urticaria) are frequently triggered by viral infections rather than allergies. Insect bite reactions, ringworm, and sun protection are also key concerns in this active, outdoors-exploring age group.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Impetigo is highly contagious — parents need clear guidance on daycare exclusion and hygiene
- Ringworm of the scalp (tinea capitis) requires oral antifungal treatment; topical creams alone are insufficient
- Not all itchy rashes are eczema — scabies, contact dermatitis, and psoriasis should be considered
- Hives with any lip/tongue swelling, difficulty breathing, or vomiting = anaphylaxis until proven otherwise
- Kawasaki disease (fever >5 days + rash + other features) has a skin component and must not be missed
- Eczema distribution changes are normal and expected as children grow — parents may think it's a new condition
