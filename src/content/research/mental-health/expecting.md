## Research Brief

Perinatal mental health affects up to 1 in 5 pregnant women, yet screening and treatment remain inconsistent. This article covers prenatal depression and anxiety (which are more common than postpartum depression), universal screening recommendations, the impact of untreated maternal mental illness on fetal development, partner mental health during pregnancy, and how to access care. ACOG's 2023 clinical practice guideline represents a major upgrade in screening standards.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: Prenatal Depression and Anxiety Are Common
- Key points: 10-20% affected, anxiety often co-occurs with depression, both are under-diagnosed
- Supported by: Source 3, Source 5, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: If you're struggling emotionally during pregnancy, you are not alone and it is not your fault

### Section 2: Why Screening Matters
- Key points: ACOG recommends universal screening, EPDS is quick and free, multiple timepoints
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: If your provider hasn't asked how you're feeling emotionally, ask them to screen you

- Key points: Cortisol pathway, preterm birth risk, neurodevelopmental effects, long-term behavioral outcomes
- Supported by: Source 3, Source 5

## Red Flags & Nuances

- In Chinese culture, mental health stigma is severe — prenatal depression is often dismissed as "想太多" (thinking too much). The article must normalize mental health care as part of prenatal care.
- Chinese families may attribute prenatal emotional distress to "not being strong enough" — this needs direct, compassionate pushback.
- Medication during pregnancy is a significant fear across cultures — the article should present the evidence honestly (some medications are well-studied and safe) without being prescriptive.
- Partner mental health is almost never addressed in Chinese prenatal care. This section may be novel information for many Chinese parents.
- Access to mental health care varies enormously by country, insurance, and language — acknowledge these barriers.
