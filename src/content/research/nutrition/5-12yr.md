## Research Brief

School-age children gain increasing autonomy over food choices — school lunches, snacks with friends, and exposure to food marketing. This article covers building balanced meals, calcium and vitamin D needs for growing bones, childhood obesity prevention without body shaming, healthy snacking, managing the school food environment, and fostering a positive relationship with food that will carry into adolescence.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- In Chinese families, academic pressure often leads to "reward foods" (考试考好了吃大餐) — this links food with achievement and can set up unhealthy patterns
- Chinese school lunch culture differs significantly from Western packed-lunch culture — address both contexts
- Weight stigma is pervasive in Chinese culture (直接说孩子胖) — the article should explicitly address why commenting on a child's weight is harmful
- "Clean your plate" culture (光盘行动) conflicts with responsive eating — navigate this with respect
- Bone broth (骨头汤) is culturally valued for bone health but doesn't provide significant calcium — address gently
