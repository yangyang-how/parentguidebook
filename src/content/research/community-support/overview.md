## Research Brief

"It takes a village" is not just a proverb — it's a research finding. Social support is one of the strongest predictors of parenting quality, parental well-being, and child outcomes. Isolated parents are at higher risk for depression, harsh parenting, and burnout. This overview covers the evidence on social support and parenting outcomes, the types of support that matter (informational, emotional, instrumental), the risk of isolation, and how to build and maintain a support network.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: Why "It Takes a Village" Is Science
- Key points: Three types of support, multiple pathways, stronger support = better outcomes
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: The support around you isn't a luxury — it's a direct determinant of how well you and your child do

### Section 2: The Danger of Isolation
- Key points: Isolation increases stress/harsh parenting, risk factor for maltreatment, pandemic lesson
- Supported by: Source 2, Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: If you feel isolated, that's not just uncomfortable — it's a risk factor. Take it seriously.

### Section 3: Building Your Village
- Key points: In-person > online (but online helps), community programs, multiple sources of support
- Supported by: Source 9, Source 6, Source 4
- Parent-facing angle: Your village doesn't build itself. You build it — one playdate, one parent group, one neighbor at a time.

### Section 4: Community-Level Protection
- Key points: Collective efficacy, neighborhood connection, community responsibility for children
- Supported by: Source 10, Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: When you look out for your neighbor's kid, you're not being nosy — you're being a village

### Section 5: Supporting Caregivers IS Supporting Children
- Key points: Caregiver well-being → child outcomes, strengthening adult capabilities, systemic support
- Supported by: Source 5, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: Taking care of yourself is not selfish. It's the most effective thing you can do for your child.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese communities have strong collectivist traditions that naturally provide some village support — leverage this
- 坐月子 is a community support tradition — acknowledge its value while noting where it may be insufficient
- Chinese immigrant families may lose their support network when moving to a new country — isolation risk is high
- WeChat groups serve as both online community and support network for Chinese parents — acknowledge this digital village
- Cultural stigma around seeking help (丢脸) may prevent Chinese parents from accessing available support
