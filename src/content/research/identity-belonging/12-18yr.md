## Research Brief

Adolescence (12–18 years) is the defining period for identity formation. Erikson's "identity vs. role confusion" stage describes teens' central task: integrating childhood experiences, emerging physical maturity, and societal expectations into a coherent sense of self. Marcia's identity status model (diffusion, foreclosure, moratorium, achievement) provides a framework for understanding where teens are in this process. Peer groups and, increasingly, social media serve as both mirrors and stages for identity exploration, while family belonging remains a protective anchor.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Identity diffusion (persistent lack of direction or commitment) in later adolescence may signal depression or other mental health concerns
- Foreclosure (adopting parents' identity without exploration) can appear "well-adjusted" but may lead to crisis later
- Social media effects on identity are not uniformly negative — context and usage patterns matter
- Identity formation extends into the mid-20s; parents should not expect "resolution" by age 18
- Racial, ethnic, sexual, and gender identity exploration may follow different timelines and processes
