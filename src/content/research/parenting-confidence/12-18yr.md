## Research Brief

Parenting a teenager can feel like parenting a stranger — the child who once sought your approval now challenges your authority, the rules that worked no longer apply, and the stakes (driving, substances, mental health) are higher than ever. This article covers teen rebellion and parental self-doubt, the art of letting go, trusting your teen, knowing when professional help is needed, and the transition to parenting an adult child.

## Suggested Outline

- Key points: Previous strategies stop working, consultative > directive, new skill set needed

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese cultural expectations of filial piety (孝道) make adolescent autonomy-seeking especially threatening to parental confidence
- "Letting go" is culturally foreign in Chinese parenting — frame as evolution, not abandonment
- Help-seeking for teen mental health is especially stigmatized in Chinese culture
- The empty nest experience may be even more intense for Chinese parents whose identity is heavily invested in child-rearing and academic success
- Bicultural Chinese-American teens may create unique confidence challenges as parents navigate conflicting cultural expectations
