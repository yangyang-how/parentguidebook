## Research Brief

Adolescent respiratory health involves managing asthma through puberty, recognizing exercise-induced bronchoconstriction (EIB), understanding the devastating effects of vaping on developing lungs, and addressing common teen ENT issues like chronic sinusitis and sleep-disordered breathing. This article covers what parents need to know about their teen's breathing and airway health.

- **Date:** 2024
- **URL:** https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557554/

**Key passages:**
> "Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction (EIB) refers to narrowing of the airways during or shortly after physical activity."

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: Asthma During Puberty
- Key points: Asthma patterns change with puberty, self-management transition, medication adherence
- Supported by: Source 5, Source 6
- Parent-facing angle: Your teen's asthma may improve or worsen — stay engaged even as they take over management

### Section 2: Exercise-Induced Breathing Difficulty
- Key points: EIB affects up to 20% of teens, symptoms, diagnosis, treatment, exercise is still good
- Supported by: Source 3, Source 4
- Parent-facing angle: If your teen wheezes during sports, it's treatable — don't let them quit

### Section 3: Vaping and Your Teen's Lungs
- Key points: Respiratory harm, worsened asthma, EVALI risk, declining fitness, peer pressure
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 2, Source 7, Source 8

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Vaping is the defining respiratory risk of this generation — parents often underestimate it because "it's not smoking"
- Many teens hide vaping from parents — dental erosion and persistent cough may be the first clues
- EIB is underdiagnosed because teens often just stop exercising instead of seeking help
- Cultural note: In China, air pollution is an additional major respiratory risk factor for teens — worth mentioning
- Asthma medication non-compliance in teens is often about not wanting to appear "different" — address the social dimension
