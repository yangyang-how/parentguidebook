## Research Brief

School-age routines become more complex — homework, after-school logistics, extracurricular management, morning independence, screen time boundaries, and family meetings all require coordination. This is when children begin to own their routines and parents shift from directing to coaching.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: Homework Routines
- Key points: Same time/place/no screens, structure > content help, reduces family conflict
- Supported by: Source 1
- Parent-facing angle: The homework routine is more important than the homework. Seriously.

### Section 2: After-School Logistics
- Key points: Quality programs, 3-6 PM risk window, home alone readiness, extracurricular sweet spot
- Supported by: Source 2, Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: Two activities they love > five activities on the resume

### Section 3: Morning Independence
- Key points: Possible by 7-8, visual checklists, gradual transfer, EF building
- Supported by: Source 4
- Parent-facing angle: The morning your child does their whole routine without being asked is the morning your investment pays off

### Section 4: Screen Time and Family Meetings
- Key points: Media plan, screen-free zones/times, weekly family meetings, child participation
- Supported by: Source 5, Source 6
- Parent-facing angle: A weekly 20-minute family meeting prevents 100 daily negotiations

### Section 5: Family Meals Still Matter
- Key points: ≥3/week, conversation quality, vocabulary growth, protective factor
- Supported by: Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: Even as schedules get crazy, protect family dinner. It's the routine that does the most.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese academic culture often means homework dominates evening routines — the homework routine guidance is especially relevant
- After-school tutoring (补习班) is a major logistical burden in Chinese families
- Family meetings may be an unfamiliar concept in Chinese families — present as practical logistics tool
- Screen time management intersects with homework technology use — boundaries are more complex
