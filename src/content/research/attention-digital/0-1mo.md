## Research Brief

For newborns (0–1 month), all major guidelines — AAP, WHO, and AACAP — unanimously recommend zero screen time. Newborn visual systems can only focus at 20–40 cm and cannot process screen content meaningfully. Background television disrupts parent-infant interaction and reduces the quality of the caregiving environment. The only possible exception is live video chatting with family members facilitated by an adult.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "No screen time" includes background TV — many parents don't realize this counts
- Video chatting with grandparents is the only AAP-approved exception for infants
- The "video deficit effect" means babies literally cannot learn from screens at this age
- Parents should not feel guilty about incidental exposure; the concern is habitual/prolonged use
- Newborn visual limitations make screens physically irrelevant — they can't see them clearly
