## Research Brief

Social-emotional development is the foundation upon which everything else is built — academic achievement, mental health, relationships, and life satisfaction all correlate more strongly with social-emotional competence than with IQ. This overview covers what social-emotional development actually means, the CASEL framework's five core competencies, developmental milestones from birth through adolescence, and the evidence linking social-emotional skills to long-term outcomes. The message for parents: these skills are learnable, teachable, and the most important thing you can nurture.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- In Chinese culture, social-emotional skills are sometimes deprioritized relative to academic achievement. The Jones et al. data showing SE skills outpredict academics is powerful for this audience.
- SEL in schools is politically contentious in some US communities — the article should focus on the universal developmental science, not school programs
- Cultural variation in emotional expression norms is real (Chinese culture values emotional restraint more than Western culture) — the article should distinguish between regulation and suppression
- "Emotional intelligence" as a concept is well-known in Chinese parenting circles (情商) — leverage this existing framework
