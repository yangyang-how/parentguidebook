## Research Brief

Adolescent sleep is a "perfect storm" — biological circadian shifts push bedtime later, early school start times force early rising, and social/academic pressures fill the gap. Most teens are chronically sleep-deprived. This article covers the biology of adolescent sleep changes, recommended amounts, school start time advocacy, the role of screens and caffeine, melatonin use, and when sleep problems need clinical attention.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "My teen is just lazy" is the most common and most harmful misconception — the circadian shift is real biology
- Melatonin supplements are widely available but should ideally be used under medical guidance for timing and dosage
- The caffeine-sleep-caffeine cycle is a trap many teens fall into — address it directly
- Weekend catch-up sleep is common but doesn't fully compensate — and creates "social jet lag"
- Cultural note: In China, competitive academic pressure means many students sleep 6 hours or less — the article should acknowledge this reality while presenting the evidence
