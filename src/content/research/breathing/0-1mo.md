## Research Brief

Newborns (0-1 month) breathe 40-60 times per minute and commonly exhibit periodic breathing — short pauses of 5-10 seconds followed by rapid breaths — which is normal and usually resolves by 6 months. Parents must learn to distinguish normal newborn breathing patterns from signs of respiratory distress (grunting, nasal flaring, retractions, cyanosis). Safe sleep practices are critical for SIDS prevention, with the AAP recommending supine position on a firm, flat surface.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Newborn breathing is SUPPOSED to sound noisy and irregular — parents need reassurance about this
- Periodic breathing is very common (78% of newborns) and is NOT apnea
- The critical distinction: periodic breathing has no color/heart rate changes; apnea does
- Nasal congestion is extremely common in newborns (obligate nose breathers) — saline drops can help
- Premature infants need special monitoring but apnea of prematurity does not equal SIDS risk
- Home apnea monitors are NOT recommended by AAP for SIDS prevention
- This topic requires balancing reassurance (most breathing variations are normal) with clear emergency criteria
