## Research Brief

In the first month of life, identity and belonging center on the nascent caregiver-infant bond. Newborns arrive biologically prepared to attach — they recognize their mother's voice and odor within hours of birth and orient preferentially toward familiar caregivers. This period corresponds to Erikson's "trust vs. mistrust" stage, where consistent, responsive care lays the foundation for the infant's emerging sense of security and belonging in the world.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Bonding is not always instant — it is normal for some parents to take weeks to feel connected
- NICU stays or medical complications can delay but do not prevent secure attachment
- Postpartum depression/anxiety in either parent can impair bonding — screening is important
- Cultural practices around newborn care vary widely; many are compatible with secure attachment
