## Research Brief

This overview research file synthesizes findings from all six stage-specific research files (0-1mo, 1-6mo, 6mo-2yr, 2-5yr, 5-12yr, 12-18yr) for the eyes domain. The overview article covers the full arc of vision development from birth through teens, key screening milestones, the amblyopia treatment window, common pediatric eye conditions, the global myopia epidemic, and when to see a doctor.

Key themes across all stages:
- Vision develops progressively from near-total blur at birth to full adult capability by the teen years
- The red reflex test (at birth and every well-child visit) is the cornerstone newborn screening tool
- Amblyopia (lazy eye) affects 1-5% of children and has a critical treatment window that closes around age 7-9
- Strabismus (2-4% of children) is the leading cause of amblyopia in young children
- Myopia is a global epidemic driven by reduced outdoor time and increased near work; outdoor time (80-120 min/day) is the strongest protective factor
- Convergence insufficiency (4-17% of school-age children) is commonly missed and mimics ADHD
- Sports eye injuries are 90% preventable with proper eyewear
- Blue light-blocking glasses have no scientific evidence supporting eye health benefits (AAO position)

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: How Vision Develops — From Blur to Full Clarity
- Newborn: 8-12 inch focus, grayscale, intermittent misalignment normal
- 1-6mo: Fix-and-follow, color vision, depth perception
- 6mo-2yr: Distance vision, eye-hand coordination
- 2-5yr: Critical window for amblyopia detection
- 5-12yr: Myopia onset, convergence insufficiency
- 12-18yr: Myopia progression, digital eye strain

### Section 2: The Screening Schedule
- Birth: Red reflex test
- 6 months: First comprehensive exam (InfantSEE)
- Ages 1-2: Instrument-based screening
- Ages 3-5: Visual acuity testing (USPSTF recommendation)
- School age/teens: Refractive error detection

### Section 3: Amblyopia — The Hidden Condition
- 1-5% prevalence, critical window closes age 7-9
- Causes: strabismus, anisometropia
- Treatment: glasses, patching (2hr = 6hr), atropine drops
- 75% achieve 20/30 or better with early treatment

### Section 4: Common Conditions at a Glance
- Strabismus (2-4%), blocked tear ducts (6-20%), congenital cataracts, retinoblastoma (90% cure), congenital glaucoma (1 in 10,000)

### Section 5: The Myopia Epidemic
- Global surge, ages 7-16 onset, outdoor time protective (80-120 min/day)
- Screen time evidence, blue light glasses debunked
- Treatment options: atropine, MiSight, ortho-K, multifocal contacts

### Section 6: Convergence Insufficiency
- 4-17% of school-age children, mimics ADHD, missed by standard screenings
- Vision therapy effective in ~80%

### Section 7: Sports Eye Safety
- 30,000 US ER visits/year, 90% preventable, polycarbonate eyewear

### Section 8: When to Talk to Your Doctor
- Red flags (white pupil, constant misalignment, no eye contact by 8 weeks)
- Yellow flags (squinting, headaches, holding things close)

## Red Flags & Nuances

- The overview should provide a comprehensive but not overwhelming picture — parents need a roadmap, not an encyclopedia
- Amblyopia is the #1 reason screening exists; give it appropriate weight
- Myopia section should empower (outdoor time, screen limits) not alarm
- Blue light glasses: state AAO position clearly without being preachy
- Convergence insufficiency deserves a callout because it's so commonly missed
- Balance between "most children are fine" and "here's what to watch for"
