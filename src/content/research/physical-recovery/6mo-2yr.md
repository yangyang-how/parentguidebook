## Research Brief

Between 6 months and 2 years postpartum, physical recovery continues well beyond what many parents expect. Physiologic changes can persist for over 1 year after delivery. Approximately 75% of women do not lose their gestational weight within one year. Persistent pelvic floor symptoms, chronic pelvic pain (affecting 1 in 6 women), musculoskeletal disorders from childcare ergonomics, and body composition changes remain significant concerns requiring ongoing attention and, for some, specialist referral.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Persistent pelvic pain is real and diagnosable — it should not be dismissed as "normal"
- Weight retention is common and influenced by sleep, stress, hormones, and breastfeeding — not just diet
- Musculoskeletal problems from childcare (back, wrists, shoulders) are under-recognized
- Second pregnancies within 18 months may compound recovery challenges
- Cultural and social pressure to "get your body back" is harmful and not evidence-based
- Some conditions (carpal tunnel, diastasis recti) may require intervention even at 1-2 years
