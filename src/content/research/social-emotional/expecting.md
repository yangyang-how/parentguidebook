## Research Brief

Social-emotional development begins before birth. Prenatal bonding — talking, singing, and emotionally connecting with the unborn baby — is associated with better postnatal attachment and more responsive caregiving. This article covers the evidence for prenatal bonding, parental emotional preparation for the relationship shift, how stress and emotional health during pregnancy affect the developing brain, and setting the stage for responsive caregiving.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Prenatal bonding research should not be used to blame mothers for pregnancy complications or child outcomes
- In Chinese culture, 胎教 (prenatal education) is a well-established concept — this article aligns with that tradition and can leverage it
- Fathers/partners may feel disconnected during pregnancy — normalizing this while encouraging involvement is important
- For parents experiencing complicated pregnancies or unwanted pregnancies, prenatal bonding may be complex — acknowledge this gently
- Maternal stress should be discussed without catastrophizing — manageable stress is normal and not harmful
