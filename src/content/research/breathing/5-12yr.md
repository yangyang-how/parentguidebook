## Research Brief

School-age children (5-12 years) can now undergo spirometry for definitive asthma diagnosis. NAEPP guidelines classify this as a distinct age group (5-11 years) with specific stepwise treatment recommendations. Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction (EIB) becomes a major concern as children participate in organized sports. Asthma management must extend to school settings with action plans, inhaler access policies, and physical education accommodations. Exercise is recommended, not restricted, for children with well-controlled asthma.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- The biggest myth to bust: "My child has asthma so they can't play sports" — the OPPOSITE is true
- Many Olympic athletes have asthma or EIB — this is an empowering message for kids
- Swimming is often recommended but chlorine sensitivity can be an issue for some
- Cold-weather sports (skiing, ice hockey, running in winter) are higher-risk for EIB
- Proper inhaler technique with spacer is still important at this age — many children use inhalers incorrectly
- School inhaler access is legally protected in all 50 states but parents should still advocate
- Montelukast has an FDA black box warning — discuss with provider
- Asthma can be underdiagnosed in children who present only with chronic cough (cough-variant asthma)
