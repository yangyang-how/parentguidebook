## Research Brief

First-time parent anxiety is near-universal, and the information overload of pregnancy only intensifies it. This article covers building confidence before the baby arrives, navigating the overwhelming flood of advice and information, maintaining flexibility in birth plans, and the paradox that preparation and uncertainty must coexist.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: Everyone Is Scared
- Key points: 60-80% of first-time parents anxious, moderate anxiety is adaptive, you're not alone
- Supported by: Source 1
- Parent-facing angle: If you're terrified about the baby coming, you're in the vast majority

### Section 2: Information Overload — Less Is More
- Key points: Too much information decreases confidence, 2-3 trusted sources, social media is the worst
- Supported by: Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: Stop googling. Pick 2 sources you trust. Ignore everything else.

### Section 3: Preparation That Actually Builds Confidence
- Key points: Hands-on > lectures, flexibility > rigid methods, practical skills, peer connections
- Supported by: Source 2, Source 5
- Parent-facing angle: The best prenatal prep is a diaper-changing demo and a friend who's honest about how hard it is

### Section 4: Hold Your Plans Loosely
- Key points: Birth preferences not requirements, rigid expectations lead to failure feelings
- Supported by: Source 4, Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: Plan. Then plan to be surprised. The surprise is where the real parenting starts.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- In Chinese culture, 月嫂 (postpartum nannies) and family support may be expected to provide confidence — this can help but also undermine self-efficacy if parents never practice independently
- Chinese parents may be more influenced by family expectations than personal confidence — address the intergenerational pressure
- Information overload from Chinese social media platforms (小红书, 宝宝树) is intense — specific guidance to limit sources is valuable
- Partners in Chinese culture may be more excluded from prenatal preparation — explicitly include them
