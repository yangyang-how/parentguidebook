# Inclusive Language Guidelines — Parent Guidebook

These guidelines apply to ALL content on parentguidebook.org — English originals, Chinese translations, and any future languages. Every reviewer in the content pipeline (fact checker, auditor, linter) should be aware of these principles.

---

## 1. Family Structure & Caregiving

**Principle:** Not every child has two parents. Families come in all forms — single parents, grandparents, foster parents, adoptive parents, same-sex parents, older siblings as caregivers, institutional care.

**Rules:**
- Use **家长** (EN: "parent" or "caregiver"), never **父母** (which literally means "father and mother")
- Do not assume who brings the child to the doctor — say "带孩子去检查" not "让妈妈带孩子去检查"
- Do not assume a two-parent household in examples or scenarios
- When the caregiver may not be a parent, use "照顾孩子的人" (the person caring for the child)
- Do not assume marital status or household composition

**Examples:**
- ❌ "父母应该注意..." → ✅ "家长应该注意..."
- ❌ "让妈妈带孩子去检查" → ✅ "带孩子去检查"
- ❌ "Ask your husband to help" → ✅ "Ask someone to help"

---

## 2. Gender Roles

**Principle:** Caregiving is not gendered. Do not default to mothers as caregivers or fathers as absent/secondary.

**Rules:**
- Do not use gendered pronouns when referring to generic caregivers
- Do not assign caregiving tasks to a specific gender
- When referring to a generic child, use "孩子" / "他们" or restructure to avoid gendered pronouns
- Do not reinforce stereotypes about who feeds, bathes, plays with, or takes children to appointments

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Mothers should check..." → ✅ "Parents should check..."
- ❌ "妈妈在喂奶时" → ✅ "喂奶时" or "哺乳时"
- ❌ "he or she" → ✅ restructure, or use "they" / "孩子"

---

## 3. Disability & Health Conditions

**Principle:** A child is not their condition. Use person-first language. Conditions are things a person has, not things a person is.

**Rules:**
- Person-first: "有弱视的孩子" not "弱视儿童" (put the child before the condition)
- Never use: 残废, 瞎子, 聋子, 傻子 — use 视力障碍, 听力障碍, 智力障碍
- Avoid "suffers from" / "受...折磨" / "深受...之苦" → use "has" / "有...的情况" / "被诊断为"
- Avoid "defect" / "缺陷" → use "condition" / "情况" or "问题"
- Avoid "normal" as the opposite of a condition → use "typical" / "常见" or "大多数孩子"
- Do not describe medical conditions as tragedies or catastrophes
- Do not use "confined to a wheelchair" → "uses a wheelchair"

**Examples:**
- ❌ "视力有缺陷的孩子" → ✅ "视力有问题的孩子"
- ❌ "the child suffers from strabismus" → ✅ "the child has strabismus"
- ❌ "正常视力的孩子" → ✅ "视力正常发育的孩子" or "大多数孩子"
- ❌ "abnormal" / "异常" as a label → ✅ describe the specific finding

## 4. Blame & Guilt

**Principle:** Parents are doing their best. Never imply a parent caused their child's condition or should have caught it earlier. Early detection is a success, not evidence of prior failure.

**Rules:**
- Never use "if only you had..." / "要是你早点..." framing
- Never attribute conditions to parenting choices unless there is direct, strong, evidence-based causation
- Explicitly state "这不是你的错" (this is not your fault) when relevant
- Frame late discovery as a systemic issue, not a personal one
- When discussing prevention, frame as empowerment, not as blame for those who didn't prevent
- Do not say "you should have noticed" — say "here's what to watch for going forward"

**Examples:**
- ❌ "If parents had caught this earlier..." → ✅ "When caught early, treatment is very effective"
- ❌ "This could have been prevented" → ✅ "Early screening helps catch this"
- ❌ "因为家长没有及时发现" → ✅ "如果能早期发现，治疗效果会更好"

## 5. Emotional Register

**Principle:** Be honest about medical realities without being dismissive or alarmist. Parents deserve accurate information delivered with compassion.

**Rules:**
- Never dismissive: avoid "不用担心" / "没什么大不了" / "don't worry about it"
- Never alarmist: avoid "非常危险！" / "太可怕了" / "This is terrifying"
- Acknowledge fear directly: "这听起来可能让人担心，这很正常"
- Convey urgency through facts, not emotion: "以天为单位，不要以周为单位"
- Do not use militaristic language: avoid "战胜疾病" / "与...作斗争" / "fight/battle/combat disease" → use "治疗" / "管理" / "应对" / "treat" / "manage"
- Do not use "victim" / "受害者" for people with medical conditions

**Examples:**
- ❌ "There's nothing to worry about" → ✅ "Most cases turn out to be benign, but it's worth checking"
- ❌ "这种病非常可怕" → ✅ "这种情况需要及时处理"
- ❌ "fight cancer" → ✅ "receive treatment for cancer"

## 6. Socioeconomic Sensitivity

**Principle:** Not all families have equal access to healthcare, transportation, insurance, or time off work. Do not assume resources.

**Rules:**
- Do not assume access to specialists, private doctors, or expensive treatments
- Provide alternatives or acknowledge barriers where possible
- Do not assume car ownership or easy transportation
- Do not assume ability to take time off work for appointments
- Do not assume internet access or health literacy levels — keep language plain
- Never label families by income: avoid "贫困家庭" / "低收入家庭" as identifiers
- When cost is relevant, state it factually without judgment

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Take your child to a pediatric ophthalmologist" (only option) → ✅ "Your child's pediatrician can do an initial check, or refer you to a specialist if needed"
- ❌ "贫困家庭的孩子更容易..." → ✅ "在医疗资源不足的地区，孩子可能..."

## 7. Cultural & Regional Sensitivity

**Principle:** Our readers span mainland China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Chinese-speaking diaspora communities worldwide. Respect all regions, healthcare systems, and cultural contexts.

**Rules:**
- Do not center one country's healthcare system as the default
- When mentioning regional practices, present them neutrally without ranking
- Respect traditional medicine — do not mock it, but be clear about evidence-based recommendations
- Do not assume simplified characters = mainland China political views
- Use neutral terms for regions: "中国大陆" / "台湾" / "香港" / "新加坡" — no political commentary
- Do not assume one "correct" way to parent across cultures
- Be aware that attitudes toward disability, mental health, and seeking help vary across cultures — do not judge

**Examples:**
- ❌ "In civilized countries, children get screened..." → ✅ "Many countries include vision screening in routine checkups"
- ❌ "Unlike traditional remedies, real medicine..." → ✅ "Evidence-based treatments that have been tested in clinical studies include..."

## 8. Age & Development

**Principle:** Children develop at different rates. Developmental variation is normal, not a failure.

**Rules:**
- Do not frame developmental differences as failures or deficits
- Use "发育速度不同" (developing at a different pace), not "发育落后" (developmentally behind)
- Avoid "normal" as the benchmark → use "typical" / "常见" / "大多数同龄孩子"
- For premature babies: always mention corrected age, explain it, normalize it
- Do not compare children to each other — compare to milestones with room for variation
- Use age ranges, not exact ages, for milestones: "大约3到4个月" not "3个月时必须能够..."
- Avoid "delayed" / "迟缓" as a label → describe what the child can and cannot yet do

**Examples:**
- ❌ "By 3 months, your baby must be able to track objects" → ✅ "Around 3 months, most babies begin to track moving objects"
- ❌ "发育迟缓的孩子" → ✅ "发育节奏较慢的孩子"
- ❌ "normal children" → ✅ "most children" or "typically developing children"

## General Principles

These overarching principles apply across all categories:

1. **Specificity over generalization** — describe specific situations, not broad groups
2. **Person-first** — the person comes before the condition, status, or characteristic
3. **Agency** — frame people as active agents, not passive recipients or victims
4. **Structural framing** — when inequities exist, name the systems and barriers, not the people
5. **Humility** — we provide information, not judgment. Parents make the decisions.
6. **Evidence-based** — every claim must be traceable to a cited source. Opinion is clearly labeled as such.

## Applying These Guidelines

- **English authors**: Follow these guidelines when drafting original content
- **Translator**: Follow these guidelines when translating EN → ZH
- **Fact Checker**: Flag any factual claims that may embed bias (e.g., unsourced claims about risk groups)
- **Auditor**: Flag any meaning drift that introduces bias not present in the original
- **Linter**: Systematically check for violations of all 8 categories above
