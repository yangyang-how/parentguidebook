## Research Brief

Pregnancy is when the work-life collision first becomes real — parental leave planning, workplace rights, financial planning for reduced income, career impact conversations, and planning the return to work all demand attention alongside the pregnancy itself. This article covers parental leave options and rights, financial planning for the transition, career impact awareness, and return-to-work planning.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese maternity leave (98 days with regional extensions) is significantly shorter than many European countries
- Chinese families may rely on grandparents rather than formal childcare for return-to-work support
- The motherhood penalty exists in China and may be more severe due to less workplace protection
- Paternal leave is minimal in China — presenting evidence for its benefits may face cultural resistance
- Financial planning differs between US and China (healthcare, education savings, housing)
