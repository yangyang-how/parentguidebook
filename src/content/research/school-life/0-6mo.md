## Research Brief

The first six months of life are a period of extraordinary brain development — more than 1 million new neural connections form every second. While formal "school" is years away, this period lays the neurological foundation for all future learning. This article covers infant stimulation through everyday interactions, tummy time as a learning activity, early brain development foundations, and how to evaluate childcare quality for infants.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese culture has a strong tradition of early academic instruction (早教) — the article should distinguish responsive enrichment from premature academic pressure
- Commercial "baby learning" products (Baby Einstein, flash cards) are aggressively marketed but not evidence-based — address this
- Grandparent caregivers in Chinese families may not be aware of serve-and-return concepts — frame as validation of natural nurturing instincts
- "Over-stimulation" is a real concern in cultures that push early development — balance the message
- Screen time for infants is common in practice despite guidelines — address without shaming
