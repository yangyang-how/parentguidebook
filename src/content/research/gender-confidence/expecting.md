## Research Brief

Gender expectations begin before birth — the moment parents learn (or choose not to learn) the sex of their baby, a cascade of assumptions, purchases, and narratives begins. This article covers gender expectations during pregnancy, the evidence on how prenatal gender knowledge affects parenting behavior, the case for reflecting on your own gender assumptions, and preparing to welcome any child regardless of how they express their gender.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Son preference (重男轻女) is declining in Chinese culture but still present — address this directly and compassionately
- Gender reveal parties are culturally Western — Chinese families may not relate but face similar expectation-setting through other means
- This article should NOT tell parents how to think about gender — it should invite reflection
- Some parents will find this topic sensitive or politically charged — lead with child development research, not political framing
- The article should be clear: this is about supporting every child, not about promoting any particular gender ideology
