## Research Brief

The first six months are when the social world ignites — social smiling, face recognition, early attachment, and the beginnings of co-regulation lay the groundwork for all future relationships. This article covers the emergence of social smiling and its significance, responsive caregiving as the foundation of secure attachment, face recognition and preference for caregivers, co-regulation (the caregiver's role in managing the infant's emotional states), and what the science of serve-and-return means in practice.

- Key points: Social from birth, face preference, social smiling at 1-2 months

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "You'll spoil the baby" is a persistent myth across cultures — the evidence is clear that responsive caregiving does not spoil babies
- In Chinese families, concerns about 宠坏 (spoiling) may lead to less responsive caregiving — address this directly
- Postpartum mental health affects co-regulation capacity — this connects to the mental health domain
- The Still Face experiment is a powerful illustration for parents but should be presented without inducing guilt about momentary inattention
- Cultural differences in physical closeness and carrying practices should be acknowledged
