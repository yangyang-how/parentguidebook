## Research Brief

Dental caries is the most common chronic childhood disease — more prevalent than asthma. This overview should help parents understand tooth development from infancy through adolescence, when to start dental care, how to prevent cavities at every age, and what warning signs need professional attention. The article covers all ages (0-18) at a high level, directing parents to age-specific articles for details.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Fluoride is politically contentious in some communities despite overwhelming scientific consensus on safety and efficacy at recommended levels. The article should present the evidence clearly without being preachy.
- Cultural consideration: In China, dental care patterns differ — many families don't take children to dentists until problems arise. The article should gently encourage preventive visits without shaming.
- "Baby teeth don't matter because they fall out" is a dangerous misconception — primary teeth maintain space for permanent teeth and untreated infection can damage developing permanent teeth beneath.
- Breastfeeding is protective overall, but prolonged nighttime breastfeeding after teeth erupt (especially with other risk factors) can contribute to ECC. This needs nuanced handling.
