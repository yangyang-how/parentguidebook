## Research Brief

From 6 months to 2 years, children undergo a dramatic leap in identity and belonging. Separation anxiety (peaking around 8–12 months) signals that the infant has formed a clear attachment and knows who "their people" are. Mirror self-recognition emerges between 15 and 24 months — a landmark in self-awareness. Erikson's second stage, autonomy vs. shame and doubt (18 months–3 years), begins in this window as toddlers assert "me do it" and test independence while still needing the secure base of belonging.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Not all children pass the mirror test at the same age — cultural factors may influence timing (some cultures do not use mirrors regularly)
- Separation anxiety that is extremely intense or persists well past 2 years may warrant evaluation
- "Autonomy" does not mean the toddler should have unlimited choice — boundaries are part of feeling safe
- Toddlers who seem to show no distress at separation may have avoidant attachment — absence of protest is not always positive
