## Research Brief

The first month postpartum is the most physically intense recovery period. The uterus involutes from 1 kg to pre-pregnancy size over 4-6 weeks, lochia progresses through three stages (rubra, serosa, alba), perineal tears heal over 4-6 weeks, and cesarean incisions require careful wound management. ACOG recommends initial contact within 3 weeks, and ERAS Society guidelines provide evidence-based cesarean recovery protocols including early ambulation and multimodal pain management.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Postpartum hemorrhage can occur up to 12 weeks postpartum (secondary hemorrhage)
- Cesarean recovery varies significantly — some women recover quickly, others take months
- "Afterpains" (uterine contractions) are stronger with subsequent births and during breastfeeding
- Cultural practices (e.g., Chinese "zuo yue zi" / sitting the month) may restrict activity — article should respect this while noting evidence
- Many women feel pressure to "bounce back" — article should normalize the lengthy recovery process
- Emotional and physical recovery are deeply intertwined in the first month
