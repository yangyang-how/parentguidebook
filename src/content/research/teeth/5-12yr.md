## Research Brief

Ages 5-12 is the mixed dentition period — baby teeth fall out while permanent teeth grow in. This is the prime window for dental sealants, the peak age for orthodontic evaluation, and when children begin taking more responsibility for their own oral hygiene. Key topics: tooth loss and replacement, sealants on first and second permanent molars, orthodontic assessment, sports mouthguards, and building independent brushing habits.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Many parents assume losing baby teeth means dental care is less important — the opposite is true, permanent teeth are permanent
- First permanent molars erupt BEHIND the baby teeth (they don't replace anything) — many parents miss them
- "My child brushes their own teeth" at age 6 usually means most surfaces are missed — supervision is still critical
- Cultural note: In China, orthodontic treatment is increasingly popular but often delayed past the optimal window — encourage the age-7 check
