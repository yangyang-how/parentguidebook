## Research Brief

Between 1 and 6 months, infants undergo rapid skeletal growth (doubling birth weight by ~5 months) while transitioning from reflexive to increasingly voluntary movement. Key motor milestones include head control (2-4 months) and rolling over (4-6 months). Tummy time is critical for both motor development and preventing positional plagiocephaly. The posterior fontanelle closes during this period (6-8 weeks). Continued vitamin D supplementation remains essential.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "Back to sleep, tummy to play" — parents sometimes avoid tummy time because baby fusses; article should normalize brief fussing and encourage persistence
- Plagiocephaly is largely cosmetic and positional, not a bone disease — but severe cases may need helmet therapy; avoid alarming parents
- Rolling timeline varies widely — some babies roll at 3 months, others not until 6+ months; the CDC milestone is what 75% achieve
- Head lag during pull-to-sit after 4 months is a red flag worth mentioning
- Container baby syndrome (too much time in car seats/bouncers) can delay motor milestones — relevant to this age
