## Research Brief

Infants aged 1-6 months remain vulnerable to respiratory illnesses, with bronchiolitis (primarily RSV) being the most common lower respiratory infection. Normal respiratory rate at this age is 30-60 breaths/minute. Periodic breathing continues but should decrease. AAP guidelines emphasize supportive care for bronchiolitis (no bronchodilators or steroids) and preventive measures including maternal RSV vaccination and nirsevimab. Safe sleep practices remain critical during the peak SIDS risk window (2-4 months).

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Bronchiolitis treatment is supportive ONLY — parents should not expect antibiotics or nebulizer treatments
- The fact that albuterol does NOT work for bronchiolitis often surprises parents who have seen it used for asthma
- Nasal suctioning before feeds is one of the most helpful things parents can do at home
- Dehydration from poor feeding is often a bigger concern than the respiratory symptoms themselves
- RSV is seasonal (winter) but other viruses cause bronchiolitis year-round
- Nirsevimab is a NEW prevention tool (approved 2023) — parents may not know about it yet
- Not every wheeze is RSV, and not every RSV causes hospitalization — most cases are mild
