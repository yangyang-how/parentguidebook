## Research Brief

Between 1 and 6 months, infants transition from indiscriminate social responsiveness to clearly preferring familiar caregivers — the earliest behavioral expression of belonging. Social smiling emerges around 2 months, laughter by 3–5 months, and by 4–6 months infants discriminate familiar from unfamiliar faces, sometimes showing early stranger wariness. This period represents Bowlby's "attachment-in-the-making" phase, where reciprocal interactions (cooing, turn-taking, mutual gaze) build the proto-identity of "I am someone who is responded to."

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Not all infants show stranger wariness at the same age — timing varies considerably
- Lack of social smiling by 3 months warrants discussion with a pediatrician
- Temperament affects how visibly an infant shows social preferences — quiet infants are not less attached
- Infants can form secure attachments to multiple caregivers (not just the mother)
