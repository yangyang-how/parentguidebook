## Research Brief

Ages 2–5 correspond to Piaget's preoperational stage, characterized by rapid language growth, symbolic play, and the development of executive function skills (working memory, inhibitory control, cognitive flexibility). This period sees dramatic gains in the ability to plan, remember rules, and regulate impulses — foundational skills for school readiness. AAP screening continues at 30 months, and the CDC provides milestones through age 5.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Egocentrism at this age is developmentally normal, not selfish behavior
- Conservation failure (thinking a tall thin glass has "more" water) is expected before age 6–7
- Executive function varies widely among preschoolers — one bad day does not indicate a problem
- Socioeconomic factors significantly impact EF development; access to quality programs helps close gaps
- Bilingual children may show EF advantages but vocabulary differences in each language
