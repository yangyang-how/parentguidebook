## Research Brief

Adolescence is when digital life becomes central — social media, gaming, smartphones, and constant connectivity. The teen brain's reward system is highly sensitive while the prefrontal cortex (impulse control) is still developing, making teens uniquely vulnerable to attention-capturing design. This article covers teen-specific digital risks, social media and mental health, gaming, developing digital autonomy, and when screen behavior crosses into problematic territory.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: The Teen Brain and Digital Design
- Key points: Reward system sensitivity, incomplete prefrontal cortex, variable reward exploitation
- Supported by: Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: Your teen isn't weak-willed — they're being targeted by design teams with PhD psychologists

- Key points: Attention fragmentation, comparison and validation seeking, FOMO, unique risks vs other media
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 4
- Parent-facing angle: Social media fragments attention differently than TV or gaming — it's the constant checking

### Section 3: Gaming — When Fun Becomes a Problem
- Key points: Normal gaming vs problematic gaming, warning signs, ADHD connection

- Key points: Screens displacing sleep, exercise, and face-to-face time, nighttime phone use
- Supported by: Source 2, Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: The real harm isn't what screens do — it's what they replace

### Section 5: Building Digital Autonomy
- Key points: Family media plans, metacognitive awareness, modeling, gradual independence
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 2, Source 8

### Section 6: When to Seek Help
- Key points: Academic decline, social withdrawal, sleep disruption, inability to disengage
- Supported by: Source 5, Source 6

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Banning social media entirely often backfires with teens — they find ways around it and lose trust
- "Digital wellness" is a growing field — there are evidence-based approaches beyond restriction
- Many teens use screens for genuine social connection, creative expression, and learning — don't pathologize all use
- Cultural note: In China, government-mandated gaming time limits exist (3 hrs/week for minors) — relevant but controversial context
