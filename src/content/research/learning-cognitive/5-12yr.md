## Research Brief

The school-age years (5–12) correspond to Piaget's concrete operational stage, during which children develop logical thinking about concrete events, master conservation, seriation, and classification, and show rapid growth in metacognition, memory strategies, and attention span. Executive function continues to mature, supporting academic achievement. This period also sees growing self-regulation and the ability to learn systematically from instruction.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Horizontal décalage means a child may "get" conservation of number but not conservation of weight — this is normal
- Formal CDC milestone checklists stop at age 5; school-age surveillance relies more on academic performance and teacher reports
- Learning disabilities (dyslexia, dyscalculia) may become apparent during this period — they reflect specific processing differences, not low intelligence
- Cultural and educational exposure heavily influence the pace of concrete operational development
