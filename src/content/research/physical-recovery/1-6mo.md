## Research Brief

The 1-6 month postpartum period involves ongoing pelvic floor rehabilitation, return to exercise, hormonal stabilization, and managing the physical toll of sleep deprivation and breastfeeding. Pelvic floor recovery can take 8+ months with consistent training. Postpartum hair loss typically begins around 3 months. Hormones generally return to baseline by 6 months, though breastfeeding prolongs elevated prolactin. Sleep deprivation significantly impacts weight retention and appetite regulation.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "Bounce back" culture is harmful — recovery takes 6-12 months minimum
- Breastfeeding does NOT guarantee weight loss; it may increase appetite via prolactin
- Sleep deprivation is a major but often overlooked physical health factor
- Pelvic floor symptoms that don't improve with training warrant specialist referral
- Postpartum thyroiditis can mimic depression or cause unexpected weight changes
- Exercise should be positioned as recovery, not punishment
