## Research Brief

By 2-5 years postpartum, parents face long-term physical consequences of childbearing that are often unrecognized. WHO reports that more than a third of women experience lasting health problems after childbirth, including pain during intercourse (35%+), low back pain (32%), urinary incontinence (8-31%), and anal incontinence (19%). This period also involves considering subsequent pregnancies, managing the physical demands of caring for toddlers/preschoolers, and addressing pregnancy-related conditions that may signal long-term cardiovascular or metabolic risk.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Many women accept chronic postpartum conditions (incontinence, pain) as "normal" — they are treatable
- The 2-5 year window is critical for cardiovascular risk screening if pregnancy complications occurred
- Physical demands of parenting (lifting 25-40 lb toddlers) cause new injuries, not just persistent postpartum ones
- Sleep disruption from toddler night waking is under-recognized as a parental health issue
- Second or third pregnancies during this window need pre-conception health optimization
- Pelvic floor physical therapy can be effective even years after delivery
