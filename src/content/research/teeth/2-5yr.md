## Research Brief

Ages 2-5 is when the full set of 20 primary teeth completes and the child begins brushing with supervision. Key topics: completing the primary dentition, transitioning from rice-grain to pea-sized toothpaste, managing thumb sucking and pacifiers, preventing ECC (the peak risk period), and preparing for the first permanent teeth.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: The Full Set — All 20 Baby Teeth
- Key points: Last molars arrive around 2.5, full set by 3, what it looks like
- Supported by: Source 6
- Parent-facing angle: A milestone worth noting — and a mouth full of teeth to protect

### Section 2: Brushing at Ages 2-5
- Key points: Switch to pea-size toothpaste at 3, parent must help, teach spitting, make it routine
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 7
- Parent-facing angle: They want to do it themselves, but they can't yet — let them "help" while you do the real brushing

### Section 3: Thumb Sucking and Pacifiers
- Key points: Stop pacifier by 3, thumb sucking usually stops naturally by 4, when to worry, interventions
- Supported by: Source 2, Source 3, Source 9
- Parent-facing angle: Don't panic — most kids stop on their own. Here's when to act.

### Section 4: Cavities at This Age — More Common Than You Think
- Key points: 11% have untreated decay, multiple teeth often affected, consequences of neglect
- Supported by: Source 4, Source 5
- Parent-facing angle: "They're just baby teeth" is the most expensive misconception in pediatric dentistry

### Section 5: Snacks, Juice, and Sugar Management
- Key points: Frequency matters more than amount, limit juice, water between meals
- Supported by: Source 4, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: It's not about never having sweets — it's about when and how often

### Section 6: What the Dentist Can Do
- Key points: Fluoride varnish, SDF for small cavities, sealants on molars, regular check-ups
- Supported by: Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: Modern dentistry has options that don't involve drilling for young children

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "They're just baby teeth" is the most common and most damaging misconception — losing baby teeth early causes space loss for permanent teeth
- SDF (silver diamine fluoride) turns cavities black — parents should be warned about the cosmetic effect but understand it's painless and effective
- Thumb sucking advice should be gentle — shaming the child makes it worse
- Cultural note: In China, dental visits for preschoolers are not routine in many families — emphasize prevention over treatment
