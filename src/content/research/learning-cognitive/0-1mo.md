## Research Brief

Newborns arrive with more cognitive capacity than previously believed. Research shows neonates can process multisensory information, prefer familiar voices, recognize patterns, and demonstrate primitive learning through habituation and classical conditioning. The first month is dominated by rapid synaptogenesis and the establishment of foundational neural networks.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Newborn "cognition" is primarily sensory-perceptual — avoid overstating reasoning abilities
- Premature infants should be assessed by adjusted age, not chronological age
- Individual variation is wide; one milestone at 1 month does not predict later intelligence
- The 0–1 month period has fewer formal CDC milestones — first checklist is at 2 months
