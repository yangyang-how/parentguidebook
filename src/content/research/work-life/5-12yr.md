## Research Brief

School-age work-life logistics shift: after-school care replaces daycare, school schedules don't match work schedules, volunteering at school feels mandatory, work travel conflicts with family events, and managing kids' activity logistics becomes a second job. This article covers these challenges and the evidence for navigating them.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: The Schedule Gap
- Key points: School hours ≠ work hours, 3-6 PM risk, after-school programs, 12+ weeks of breaks
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 2
- Parent-facing angle: The American school schedule was designed for an agrarian society. You're not imagining the mismatch.

### Section 2: School Involvement Without Volunteering
- Key points: Home involvement = school involvement, homework support, educational conversations
- Supported by: Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: You don't need to be the class parent. Reading with your child at home counts just as much.

### Section 3: Managing Activities Logistics
- Key points: 2-3 activities max, transportation burden, over-scheduling harms
- Supported by: Source 5
- Parent-facing angle: Your child doesn't need 5 activities. They need 2 they love and a parent who isn't exhausted from driving.

### Section 4: Work Travel and Absence
- Key points: Effects mediated by other parent, preparation/communication/reunion, modeling career
- Supported by: Source 4, Source 6
- Parent-facing angle: Business trips are hard. A FaceTime at bedtime and a warm hug at the door make them manageable.

### Section 5: Protecting Family Time
- Key points: Family dinners 3+/week, breakfast counts, integration strategies, non-negotiable times
- Supported by: Source 7, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: Pick your non-negotiables. For most families, it's dinner and bedtime. Protect those two.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese after-school culture includes extensive tutoring (补习班) which adds to logistics burden
- School volunteering culture differs between US and Chinese schools
- Work travel is common in Chinese business culture — specific coping strategies needed
- Family dinner is already culturally valued in Chinese families — leverage this
