## Research Brief

The 1-6 month period sees the most rapid sleep consolidation in a child's life, with the largest increase in self-regulated sleep length occurring between months 1 and 4. By 3 months, about 50% of infants sleep through the night by some criteria. Circadian rhythm develops around 6-8 weeks. The "4-month sleep regression" reflects genuine sleep architecture maturation. Behavioral sleep interventions show evidence of safety and effectiveness from around 5-6 months but are less well-supported before that age. Night waking drops sharply from 1-3 months then stabilizes.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "Sleeping through the night" has no standard definition — some studies use 5 hours, others 8 hours — manage parent expectations
- Sleep training is a personal choice, not a medical necessity — present the evidence neutrally
- Sleep training before 4-5 months is not well-supported by evidence — infant biology matters
- Breastfed babies may wake more often than formula-fed — this is biologically normal, not a problem
- 36-45% of parents report infant sleep disturbances — parents should know they are not alone
- Maternal mental health and infant sleep are closely linked — addressing one often helps the other
- Cultural attitudes toward sleep training vary significantly — avoid prescriptive tone
