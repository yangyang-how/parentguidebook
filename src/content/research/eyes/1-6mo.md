## Research Brief

Between 1 and 6 months of age, infant vision undergoes rapid development — from basic light response to coordinated binocular tracking and emerging color vision. AAP guidelines recommend red reflex testing and external eye inspection at every well-child visit during this period. Early detection of conditions like congenital cataracts, strabismus, and retinoblastoma is critical because treatment outcomes decline sharply with age.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Intermittent eye crossing is NORMAL before 2-3 months — parents should not be alarmed
- CONSTANT eye deviation at ANY age is always abnormal
- White pupil (leukocoria) is a medical emergency — could indicate retinoblastoma
- Family history of retinoblastoma requires immediate ophthalmology referral regardless of red reflex result
- Premature infants need retinopathy of prematurity (ROP) screening — separate from routine well-child screening
- The article should emphasize that most variations are normal but provide clear "see a doctor" criteria
