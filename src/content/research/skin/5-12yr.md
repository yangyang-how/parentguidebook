## Research Brief

School-age children (5-12 years) face skin conditions driven by school and sports environments: fungal infections (ringworm, athlete's foot), parasitic infestations (head lice peak at 4-11 years, scabies), and viral warts. Eczema may persist but shifts to flexural areas. Sun protection is critical — childhood sunburns increase lifelong skin cancer risk. This age group also develops greater body awareness, and visible skin conditions can affect self-esteem.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Scalp ringworm (tinea capitis) REQUIRES oral antifungal — topical alone is insufficient
- Head lice do not spread disease and are not related to hygiene — reduce stigma
- AAP does not support mandatory "no-nit" school policies — they cause unnecessary school absence
- Warts near eyes, genitals, or that are painful/rapidly growing warrant medical evaluation
- Eczema herpeticum remains a risk for school-age children with eczema — any sudden worsening with clustered blisters needs urgent care
- Sports-specific skin infections (wrestling: herpes gladiatorum; swimming: molluscum) need sport-specific guidance
- Permethrin resistance in lice is increasing — dimethicone-based products are an alternative
