## Research Brief

The period from 6 months to 2 years marks the second half of Piaget's sensorimotor stage, during which infants develop object permanence, begin symbolic thought, demonstrate means-end reasoning, and acquire language at a rapid pace. This is also when the AAP recommends formal developmental screening (at 9 and 18 months). Brain growth remains exceptionally rapid, with toddlers' brains reaching approximately 80% of adult volume by age 2.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- The A-not-B error is normal at 8–12 months — it is not a sign of delay
- Language milestones vary widely; bilingual children may have smaller vocabularies in each language but normal total vocabulary
- Screen time recommendations are firm for under 18 months — video chat is the exception
- "Late talkers" who understand language well may catch up, but should still be screened
