## Research Brief

Digital media use among children has grown dramatically, raising concerns about effects on attention, executive function, sleep, and social development. The AAP, WHO, and major research bodies have issued age-specific guidelines. Evidence shows associations between excessive screen time and attention problems, though causation is complex. Current guidance emphasizes content quality, co-viewing, and balance over strict time limits alone.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Screen time does NOT cause ADHD — it can mimic or worsen attention symptoms
- The evidence base is mixed: some large cohort studies find little-to-no association
- Quality and context matter more than raw hours — educational co-viewing differs from passive consumption
- AAP has shifted away from rigid time limits toward a holistic "family media plan" approach
- WHO guidelines are more prescriptive (zero screens under 2) than the updated AAP approach
