## Research Brief

When children are school-age (5-12), parents are years past childbirth but still dealing with long-term physical consequences. Pelvic floor disorders may emerge or worsen 5-10+ years postpartum, especially as perimenopause approaches. This stage also presents an opportunity: as children become more independent, parents can prioritize their own exercise, preventive health screenings, and management of chronic conditions. Research shows parental physical activity directly influences children's activity levels, making self-care a family health strategy.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- New onset urinary incontinence or prolapse symptoms years after childbirth are treatable — not inevitable
- Perimenopause can begin in the late 30s-40s, coinciding with children's school years
- Parents often neglect their own health during these busy years — article should frame self-care as essential, not selfish
- Pregnancy history should be shared with primary care providers for ongoing risk assessment
- Family exercise benefits both parent recovery and child development
- Weight-bearing exercise becomes increasingly important for bone health as perimenopause approaches
