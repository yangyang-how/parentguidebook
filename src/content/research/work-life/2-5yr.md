## Research Brief

The preschool years bring unique work-life logistics — preschool costs rival college tuition, managing sick days becomes a constant juggle, career advancement with young children at home is challenging, and shared parenting responsibilities need ongoing negotiation.

## Sources

- **Date:** 2024
- **URL:** https://www.childcareaware.org/
- **Type:** government-publication

**Key passages:**
> "The average cost of center-based preschool in the US exceeds the average cost of in-state college tuition in most states."

**Claims supported:**

- 10-20% of household income

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: The Cost of Preschool
- Key points: Exceeds college tuition, 10-20% of income, quality is development not babysitting
- Supported by: Source 1, Source 5
- Parent-facing angle: Yes, preschool really does cost more than college in most states. No, that's not okay.

### Section 2: The Sick Day Problem
- Key points: 6-8 illnesses/year, backup plans, major conflict source
- Supported by: Source 2
- Parent-facing angle: Your child will be sick 6-8 times this year. Have a backup plan before the first fever.

### Section 3: Career and Parenthood
- Key points: Preschool penalty, maintain investment, flexibility > ambition reduction
- Supported by: Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: You don't have to choose between career and parenthood. But the system makes it feel that way.

### Section 4: Sharing the Load
- Key points: Equal sharing improves outcomes, mental load problem, regular check-ins, gender gap
- Supported by: Source 4, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: Doing the laundry is not "helping." It's parenting. Both parents do it.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese families often solve the preschool cost problem with grandparent care — different calculus
- Sick day management may fall to grandparents in Chinese families
- Career penalty for Chinese mothers may be even more severe due to workplace culture
- Mental load discussion is relatively new in Chinese parenting discourse — introduce the concept
