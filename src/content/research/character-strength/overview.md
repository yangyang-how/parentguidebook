## Research Brief

Character strength in children encompasses moral development, prosocial behavior, and the cultivation of virtues like courage, kindness, perseverance, and fairness. The VIA Classification (Peterson & Seligman, 2004) identifies 24 character strengths organized under six virtues — wisdom, courage, humanity, justice, temperance, and transcendence. Research shows these strengths are observable even in very young children and predict well-being, academic success, and social competence across development. Character develops through relationships, play, modeling, and guided practice.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- "Character education" can become moralistic lecturing if not rooted in relationships and modeling
- Character strengths are culturally influenced — different cultures may emphasize different virtues
- Expecting advanced moral reasoning from young children ignores developmental readiness
- Prosocial behavior and moral understanding develop at different rates
- Children who appear "too good" (excessively compliant) may be suppressing needs rather than showing genuine character
