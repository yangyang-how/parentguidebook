## Research Brief

Ages 12-18 brings the completion of permanent dentition (except wisdom teeth), peak orthodontic treatment years, and new risk factors: eating disorders, energy/sports drinks, tobacco/vaping, and declining parental oversight. This article covers wisdom teeth, orthodontic care, teen-specific cavity risks, the eating disorder–dental erosion connection, and transitioning to adult dental responsibility.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Eating disorders and dental health is a sensitive intersection — the article should provide information without triggering shame or avoidance
- Vaping/e-cigarettes are a newer oral health risk not fully addressed in older guidelines — worth mentioning
- Energy drinks are marketed to teens and are devastating for enamel — this deserves emphasis
- Wisdom teeth extraction is not always necessary — avoid presenting it as inevitable
- Cultural note: In China, orthodontic treatment is increasingly sought for aesthetic reasons; the article should also emphasize functional benefits
