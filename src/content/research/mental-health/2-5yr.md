## Research Brief

The preschool years are when emotional disorders first become distinguishable from normal developmental behavior — but telling the difference is genuinely hard. This article covers tantrums vs emotional disorders, anxiety in preschoolers (more common than most parents realize), behavioral signs of distress, the impact of trauma and adverse childhood experiences (ACEs), when tantrums signal something more, and the emerging evidence for play therapy and parent-child interaction therapy.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- In Chinese culture, behavioral problems in young children are often attributed to "lack of discipline" (管教不够) rather than emotional needs — this frames intervention as parenting failure
- ACEs conversation must acknowledge that intergenerational trauma is particularly relevant for immigrant families navigating cultural identity
- Play therapy may be unfamiliar to Chinese families who expect more structured, directive interventions — explain why play IS the therapy
- PCIT coaching approach may conflict with Chinese authoritative parenting norms — frame it as enhancing (not replacing) existing parenting
- The line between cultural parenting practices and adverse experiences requires cultural humility
