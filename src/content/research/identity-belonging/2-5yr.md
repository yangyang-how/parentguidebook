## Research Brief

Between ages 2 and 5, children develop the "categorical self" — describing themselves in concrete terms (age, gender, size, abilities) — and begin to form social identities through play and peer interaction. Erikson's initiative vs. guilt stage (3–6 years) centers on whether children feel confident to plan, explore, and lead, or guilty and inhibited. This period also sees rapid growth in perspective-taking, emotional labeling, and the ability to claim membership in social groups ("I'm a big kid," "I'm in the butterfly class").

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Preschoolers' self-descriptions are all-or-nothing ("I'm the fastest!") — this is normal, not narcissism
- Children who consistently avoid new activities or say "I can't" may be experiencing excess guilt
- Cultural background shapes which categories children use to describe themselves
- Race and ethnicity awareness begins in this period — children notice physical differences by age 3–4
