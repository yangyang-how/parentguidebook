## Research Brief

School-age community expands to include the school itself, sports and activity parent networks, neighborhood relationships, volunteering as community-building, and teaching children their role in community. This article covers the school community, building parent networks through activities, neighborhood safety and connection, and fostering children's community responsibility.

## Suggested Outline

### Section 1: The School Community
- Key points: Parent networks, information flow, advocacy, especially valuable for immigrants
- Supported by: Source 1
- Parent-facing angle: Get to know the other parents at school. Your child's education is partly a team effort.

### Section 2: Activity Communities
- Key points: Sports/music/scout parents, activity communities become broader support
- Supported by: Source 2
- Parent-facing angle: The parent you bond with on the soccer sideline might become the friend you call at midnight

### Section 3: Neighborhood and Safety
- Key points: Collective efficacy, knowing neighbors, shared norms, intervention willingness
- Supported by: Source 3
- Parent-facing angle: Know your neighbors. Watch out for their kids. Let them watch out for yours.

### Section 4: Teaching Community Responsibility
- Key points: Family volunteering, modeling engagement, prosocial identity, childhood → adult engagement
- Supported by: Source 4, Source 7, Source 8
- Parent-facing angle: Want to raise a good citizen? Show them what one looks like.

### Section 5: Cultural Community and Support Networks
- Key points: Cultural community for identity, reciprocal support, buffer against family stress
- Supported by: Source 5, Source 6
- Parent-facing angle: The community that holds you up also holds your child up

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Chinese school communities may center on academic competition — broaden to include supportive functions
- Activity-based communities (补习班 parent groups) are a Chinese cultural variant — recognize as valid community
- Neighborhood community varies enormously between Chinese and American contexts
- Cultural community (Chinese school, Chinese church, cultural associations) remains important at this age
- Reciprocal support may need explicit encouragement in cultures where accepting help feels like weakness
