## Research Brief

Between 1 and 6 months, infants develop increasing visual acuity and attentional control, but remain unable to learn meaningfully from screen content. The "video deficit effect" persists throughout this period. All major guidelines maintain a zero-screen recommendation. Background media continues to disrupt the quality of parent-infant interactions and may reduce the verbal input critical for language development. Parental co-viewing with commentary may partially offset negative effects, but direct interaction remains superior.

## Red Flags & Nuances

- Babies may appear fascinated by screens (bright colors, movement) but this is not learning
- "Video deficit effect" means babies learn less from screens than from identical live demonstrations
- Background TV is an often-overlooked form of screen exposure that counts toward impact
- Parents should not feel judged — the goal is awareness, not perfection
- Brief, incidental exposure is different from habitual/daily screen routines
